## Understanding per-platform pricing
Per-platform pricing means charging different rates for different social media platforms. For example, you might charge more for a YouTube video than for an Instagram post. This is because each platform has its own unique characteristics and requirements. YouTube videos, for example, require more production time and effort than Instagram posts. Here are some factors to consider when determining your per-platform pricing:
1. Production time and effort
2. Platform-specific requirements (e.g. YouTube video length, Instagram post format)
3. Audience engagement and reach
4. Brand requirements and expectations

Let's consider another example. Suppose you're creating content for TikTok, and you charge Rs 20,000 for a TikTok video. However, you also create content for Facebook, and you charge Rs 30,000 for a Facebook post. This is an example of per-platform pricing, where you're charging different rates for different platforms.

To determine your per-platform pricing, follow these steps:
1. Research your target audience and their preferred platforms
2. Analyze the production time and effort required for each platform
3. Consider the platform-specific requirements and brand expectations
4. Set your pricing tiers based on these factors

## Understanding per-format pricing
Per-format pricing means charging different rates for different content formats within a platform. For example, you might charge more for an Instagram Carousel than for a single Instagram post. This is because each format has its own unique characteristics and requirements. Instagram Carousels, for example, require more planning and effort than single posts. Here are some factors to consider when determining your per-format pricing:
1. Content complexity and production time

3. Audience engagement and reach
4. Brand requirements and expectations

Let's consider another example. Suppose you're creating Instagram Reels, and you charge Rs 25,000 for a simple Reel. ## India tier vs metro premium pricing
India tier pricing refers to pricing for brands that target a national audience, while metro premium pricing refers to pricing for brands that target a specific metropolitan area (e.g. Mumbai, Delhi). Metro premium pricing is often higher than India tier pricing, as it targets a more specific and affluent audience. Here are some factors to consider when determining your India tier vs metro premium pricing:
1. Target audience and reach
2. Brand requirements and expectations
3. Competition and market rates
4. Your unique value proposition and expertise

Let's consider an example. Suppose you're a creator with a national audience, and you charge Rs 50,000 for a sponsored post. However, you also work with brands that target a specific metropolitan area, such as Mumbai, and you charge Rs 70,000 for a sponsored post. This is an example of India tier vs metro premium pricing, where you're charging different rates for different target audiences.

To determine your India tier vs metro premium pricing, follow these steps:
1. Research your target audience and their location
2. Analyze the brand requirements and expectations for each target audience
3. Consider the competition and market rates for each target audience
4. Set your pricing tiers based on these factors
